PlanAtMO description

PlanAtMO is a 1D spherically symmetric coupled magma ocean-atmosphere evolution model 
recently designed to study and compare the effect of equilibrium  or non-equilibrium 
volatile outgassing on planetary evolution.

The thermal evolution of the planet composed of a silicate envelope and a metallic core 
is similar to that of other coupled models and accounts for the mixing effect of volatiles.

The cases considered here correspond to the commonly assumed equilibrium 
between the atmosphere and the magma ocean. However, contrary to the aforementioned works, 
PlanAtMO considers that when a shallow magma ocean and overlying a mushy mantle coexist, 
the heat loss at the surface of the planet only contributes to the cooling of the
magma ocean, because the latter convects much faster than the sluggish mushy or solid 
mantle underneath it. This typically results in considerably faster  magma ocean 
solidification. Heat transfer in the atmosphere can be accounted for 
by a purely radiative gray model. The thermal contribution of the core 
is neglected. Recently, a gray or k-correlated radiative-convective heat transfer model 
radconv1d by for H2O, N2, and CO2 species has been added in addition to 
the purely radiative gray model . 

In this study, we will exclusively rely on the gray  radiative-convective atmospheric heat 
transfer model, with CO2 and H2O, and a small amount of background N2 (1 bar). Radiogenic 
heat is accounted for, using the same concentrations and decay constants for heat producing 
elements (26Al, 60Fe, 40K, 232Th, 235U,238U) as in {nikolaou2019factors} (their Table 7). 
However, as pointed out in {nikolaou2019factors}, the effect of this heat source is 
negligible for Cases~1-3 due to the relatively short time spanned prior to solidification 
and the high value of outgoing convective heat flux out of the magma ocean.

PlanAtMO is written in Fortran, and has been successfully benchmarked against three 
distinct implementations of the same model in  Matlab and Python. The model detailed 
description can be found in {Walbecq.etal2025}.
