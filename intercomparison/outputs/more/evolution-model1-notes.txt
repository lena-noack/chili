MORE calculates the freezing of the magma ocean via the assumed decrease in melt fraction of the magma ocean, without considering an actual time evolution or cooling at the surface.
In principle this should deliver similar results to time-dependent evolution models as long as no time-dependent atmospheric escape is considered.

Melt temperature is calculated at 40% between solidus and liquidus, following Morschhauser et al. 2011 (Takahashi 1990) for p<10 GPa, Stamenkovic et al. 2011 for p<100 GPa, 
and Stixrude 2014 for higher pressures, with interpolations at the pressure transitions to avoid melt temperature jumps.
During freezing, we assume that always 1% of the melt is trapped in the solidified volume per freezing step.
