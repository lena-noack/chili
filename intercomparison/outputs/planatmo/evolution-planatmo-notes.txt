\section{\tt PlanAtMO}

 {\tt PlanAtMO} is a 1D spherically symmetric coupled magma ocean-atmosphere evolution model recently designed to study and compare the effect of equilibrium  or non-equilibrium volatile outgassing on planetary evolution \citep{salvador2023convective,Walbecq.etal2025}.

The thermal evolution of the planet composed of a silicate envelope and a metallic core is similar to that of other coupled models  \citep{lebrun2013thermal,salvador2017relative,nikolaou2019factors}, and  accounts for the mixing effect of volatiles \citep{bower2019linking}. 
The cases considered here correspond to the commonly assumed equilibrium between the atmosphere and the magma ocean. 
However, contrary to the aforementioned works, {\tt PlanAtMO} considers that when a shallow magma ocean and overlying a mushy mantle coexist, 
the heat loss at the surface of the planet only contributes to the cooling of the
magma ocean, because the latter convects much faster than the sluggish mushy or solid mantle underneath it. 
This typically results in considerably faster  magma ocean solidification \citep{Walbecq.etal2025}. 
Heat transfer in the atmosphere can be accounted for by a purely radiative gray model \cite{elkins2008linked}. The thermal contribution of the core is neglected.
Recently, a gray or k-correlated radiative-convective heat transfer model {\tt radconv1d} by \citet{Marcq.etal2017} for H$_2$O, N$_2$, and CO$_2$ species has been added in addition to the purely radiative gray model \cite{elkins2008linked}.
In this study, we will exclusively rely on the gray  radiative-convective atmospheric heat transfer model, with CO$_2$ and H$_2$O, and a small amount of background N$_2$ (1 bar).
Radiogenic heat is accounted for, using the same concentrations and decay constants for heat producing elements ($^{26}$Al, $^{60}$Fe, $^{40}$K, $^{232}$Th, $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U) as in \citet{nikolaou2019factors} (their Table~7). However, as pointed out in \citet{nikolaou2019factors},
the effect of this heat source is negligible for Cases~1-3 due to the relatively short time spanned prior to solidification and the high value of outgoing convective heat flux out of the magma ocean.

{\tt PlanAtMO} is written in Fortran, and has been successfully benchmarked against three distinct implementations of the same model in  Matlab 
and Python. 
The model detailed description can be found in \citet{Walbecq.etal2025}.
